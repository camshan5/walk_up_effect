Our Awesome Team

Mission Driven. Story Tellers. Hope Providers.

Brandon Gross

Founder & CEO

Brandon was born and raised in Southern California, where he quickly developed a love of sports and water. At a young age, Brandon jumped into the sport of water polo and quickly fell in love with the game.

Fast forward several years, Brandon is playing college water polo at California Lutheran University in Thousand Oaks, CA. During his time in college, Brandon decided to give coaching a shot which led to a new passion... teaching!

Upon graduation, he jumped into the education world and became a 7th-grade algebra teacher and athletic director. After a few years of teaching, Brandon took a job as a director at Apex Leadership Co. Apex is a company that contracts with schools throughout the United States to teach a unique program focused on valuable leadership skills for students. Additionally, Apex Leadership offers a service that utilizes its staff and the students who have taken the leadership course, to assist the school in raising contributions from the families and community for the school.

Brandon has a love for helping others be their best selves and reach their full potential. While on his honeymoon, Brandon received the heartbreaking news of a mass shooting at a bar in the town he attended college. This news led Brandon to develop the idea of The Walk Up Effect.

Brandon brings the vision behind the company that will help provide the youth with the resources and strength they need to overcome mental health struggles.

Trevor was born in Seattle, Washington but grew up most of his life in a small, central California farm town. Growing up, he not only had a strong passion for playing sports but also music, which led him to learn how to play the drums. Although he played basketball and did Track & Field, football is where he truly shined and became most passionate eventually leading him to play at the college level at California Lutheran University in Thousand Oaks, CA.

During his time in college, he got linked up with an incredible organization called Apex Leadership Company, in which he now co-owns and operates, serving elementary and middle school all over the greater Los Angeles and Orange County area.

Apex Leadership Co. brings young, energetic individuals (many ex-college athletes) who are passionate about empowering youth onto school campuses and teaches students impactful leadership lessons, helps them develop positive character attributes, and raises the school much-needed funds.

Trevor is the oldest of 5 siblings whose mother has been taking in foster children through the foster care system for the last 15 years. To say he has had a strong heart for young people is an understatement as he has been involved in children's lives who have been in the most unimaginable and tragic of circumstances.

For Trevor, that fire for young people burns stronger now than ever knowing there are so many young people struggling with mental health, including family members. Like Brandon, Trevor too was devastated and touched in a major way after receiving the news of the locally-known Thousand Oaks establishment, Borderline Bar and Grill, being the site of a mass shooting. Having friends that were present during this tragedy and losing a couple of classmates was something they never thought they'd experience.

With The Walk Up Effect, Trevor hopes to bring awareness to mental health in young people, educate them on how they can help their peers and, provide those struggling with the resources to overcome, and in turn, live a fulfilled life.

Joseph Sparks

Mental Health Director

Joseph Sparks is an Associate Clinical Social Worker specializing in individuals of all ages and fathers of children with substance use disorder. Like Brandon, Joseph grew up in Southern California and the two of them have been best friends since they were 10 years old. They eventually would go to middle school and high school together where they helped Agoura High win the school's first water polo CIF championship.

Upon graduating high school, Joseph, like many of the students, struggled with substance abuse. With proper care, treatment, and courage, Joseph is now approaching 10 years clean and sober. Joseph then went on to receive his Bachelor of Arts degree in Psychology from California State University Channel Islands and received his Masters of Social Work degree from the University of Colorado Denver.

Joseph has the experience and a passion for working with individuals struggling with substance use, anxiety, and depression. Not only does he bring knowledge on everything mental health, but he brings the first-hand experience to The Walk Up Effect that brings an insight on how to correctly approach struggling individuals.

Over the past decade, Joseph has worked in different levels of care for addiction and mental health treatment. Joseph utilizes evidence-based practices including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT), Solution Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT), and Motivational Interviewing (MI), to help his clients process emotions, identify their strengths, and overcome challenges.

For more information about Joseph's experience, practice, and treatments, visit: josephsparkstherapy.com
